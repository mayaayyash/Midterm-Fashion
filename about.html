Our story

Masquerade started as a design experiment in a tiny studio apartment. I was frustrated with fast fashion and how quickly clothes felt “old”. Instead of buying more, I wanted a single piece that could keep changing with me.

The first Masquerade prototype was a black dress with a detachable skirt. With a few zippers and buttons, it became a top, a midi dress, and an evening gown. Friends kept asking, “Wait…is that the same dress?”

Our mission

Masquerade is about flexibility, creativity, and sustainability. Transformable pieces mean:

  • Buying fewer items, but wearing them in more ways.
  • Expressing different sides of yourself with one wardrobe.
  • Keeping clothing in use longer instead of throwing it away.

About the designer

My name is Maya Ayyash, and I am a fashion and web design student. I am inspired by theater costumes, masks, and the idea that one person can play many roles. Masquerade is my way of bringing that concept into everyday clothing.
